The typeface Roboto was created by Christian Robertson and was available for use in 2011. Christian Robertson, an in-house designer at Google, designed Roboto as a replacement for Droid, the Google font that was used for the Android system.

Roboto was meant to have an excellent appearance on different screens. Roboto allows letters to take up as much space as it needs and ultimately, making for an improved experience for the reader.
